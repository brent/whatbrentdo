Here's a little more about me

I'm a New Yorker—born and raised. I left to go to college in Philly and came right back. When The City calls you answer her.

As a kid, I carried pencils, a red clipboard, and a stack of paper everywhere. I used to draw like people use phones now. I taught myself how to use a computer in the early-mid-late-ish 90s. Not too long after that, I learned how to use creative software. During that time, scanners were magic to me.

As I got older and spent more time with the web, I made more digital things and didn't use the scanner so much. The internet changed how and why I created. It gave me new ways—and things—to create that I couldn't dream of when I was carrying that red clipboard around.

And I had a pretty ridiculous imagination.
